# Code of Conduct

## Introduction
We are committed to creating a safe, welcoming and productive environment for everyone. Our goal is to collaborate effectively with open communication, positive impact and respecting each other. This code of conduct outlines our values, expectations, and procedures to ensure a supportive and professional learning experience for all team members.

## A Statement on diversity and inclusivity

We value diversity and are committed to creating an inclusive environment that respects individuals of all backgrounds, identities, and experiences. This includes differences in race, ethnicity, nationality, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, ability, age, academic background and many more. We believe that diverse perspectives strengthen our work and contribute to better learning outcomes.

## Expected Behaviour

All participants are expected to:

- Treat others with respect, kindness, and professionalism

- Communicate openly, honestly, and constructively

- Be inclusive and considerate of different viewpoints and experiences

- Provide and receive feedback in a respectful and supportive manner

- Contribute fairly to the project and meet agreed-upon responsibilities and deadlines

- Help create a positive, collaborative team environment

## Unaccepted Behaviour

The following behaviours are considered unacceptable:

- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusion of any kind

- Offensive, abusive, or disrespectful language or actions

- Bullying, intimidation, or personal attacks

- Disruptive behavior that negatively affects group collaboration

- Plagiarism, and any form of academic misconduct

- Repeated failure to communicate or contribute without valid justification

## List of Consequences for Unacceptable Behaviour

If unacceptable behaviour occurs, the following steps may be taken depending on severity:

- Discuss the issue respectfully in the group

- Set clear expectations for future

- If the problem continues, contact the teaching team

## Procedure for Reporting and Dealing with Unacceptable Behaviour

If a team member experiences or witnesses unacceptable behaviour, they should:

- Raise and address the concern within the group
- If the issue is serious or unresolved, contact and seek assistance from the teaching team
